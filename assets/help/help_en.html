ShashGui User Manual: Beyond the Eval

Welcome to ShashGui Mobile. Unlike traditional chess interfaces that simply show a cold numerical evaluation (e.g., +1.50), ShashGui is a true portable laboratory designed to understand chess. The goal isn't to tell you who is winning, but why and how to continue playing, translating engine thought into human concepts via Alexander Shashin's Theory.

⚠️ WARNING - FUNDAMENTAL CLOUD REQUIREMENT:

ShashGui Mobile is designed as a lightweight, high-performance client. To ensure the proper functioning of the Oracle (LiveBook) and extended evaluations, an active Internet connection is required. Massive analyses and heavy database saving operations are delegated to our Cloud servers.

📚 Shashin's Theory: The Physics of Chess

At the core of the entire system is the framework developed by Russian physicist and master Alexander Shashin. Shashin breaks down each position by measuring fundamental factors: Material, Time, Safety, Compactness, and Space. Based on the thermodynamic balance between these elements, the position is classified into one of three strategic "states", embodied by the styles of three legendary World Champions:

  • 🔥 Mikhail Tal (Attack / Expansion):
    When there is a dynamic advantage or great chaos on the board. The goal is to maximize tension and seek tactical complications to destroy opponent defenses.
  • ⚖️ José Raúl Capablanca (Strategy / Equilibrium):
    When the position is stable and perfectly balanced. The goal is the accumulation of small advantages, harmonious centralization, and precise, risk-free positional maneuvering.
  • 🛡️ Tigran Petrosian (Defense / Contraction):
    When under attack or at a slight disadvantage. The goal is absolute prophylaxis, building impregnable fortresses, and neutralizing the opponent's initiative.

📱 The Laboratory: Tools at your disposal

ShashGui's mobile interface is divided into functional areas designed for touch use:

1. The Board and Controls

  • Interaction: Drag or tap pieces to move.
  • Navigation: Use the gray bar below the board to scroll back and forth through the move tree. The blue button with two arrows flips the board orientation.
  • Visual FEN Editor (🧩): Tapping the green grid icon in the bottom bar opens a full editor to set custom positions by placing pawns and pieces manually.

2. The Engine and Analysis

Use the bottom bar to power on the engine (Alexander or ShashChess). Once on, you will unlock advanced features:

  • ⚙️ Settings: Open internal engine options (e.g., Hash, Threads, LimitStrength).
  • 🧠 Continuous Analysis: Launches the 2-Phase State Machine. The engine will first determine the Shashin Zone (e.g., Tal) and then adapt its calculation "personality" to explore it.
  • ⚔️ Play (Handicap): Challenge the engine directly on your phone by setting a time control (Fischer or fixed) and an Elo limit to simulate a human opponent.

3. The Cloud LiveBook (Oracle)

Located below the board, the LiveBook queries the largest global databases in real-time:

  • Lichess Masters: Shows the moves historically played most by human Grandmasters. Ideal for studying openings.
  • Smart Import (🔗): Tapping the blue chain icon lets you paste a Lichess.org game link or a PGN string. The app will automatically download and build the move tree for you to explore!
💡 Win Probability and Arrows:
As you navigate the tree, you'll notice ShashGui doesn't show a cold "+1.50" score, but a win percentage (e.g., 72% Tal). The arrows on the board automatically take the color of the detected thermodynamic zone (e.g., Green for advantage, Yellow/Orange for defense).

⚔️ Crossed Evaluation (Coach)

An advanced educational tool (green crossed-arrows icon 🔀). It doesn't just point out the best move, but acts as a bridge between your intuition and a mentor's judgment.

  • How it works: Enter your estimated Elo. The system will assign the appropriate "Master" (from a limited Alexander for beginners, to pure ShashChess for Experts).
  • The Dialogue: The Student proposes tactical plans, and the Master evaluates them, returning a clear written report explaining whether the idea is good or yields a structural advantage.

⚠️ Threat Detection (Threat Scanner)

Often when analyzing, we only focus on our own plans. The Threat Scanner (red crosshair icon 🎯) executes a "Null Move" algorithm: it forcibly passes the turn to the opponent to discover what threats they have in store. If it detects a dangerous attack, it will draw a deep red arrow on the board!
